Add salt to boiling pasta water.

Adding pasta water to pasta sauce helps emulsify the sauce.

Prepare all ingredients before starting to cook.

Cleaning as you cook will reduce the amount of cleaning at the end of the meal.

A falling knife HAS NO HANDLE. DO NOT TRY TO CATCH IT

Keep your knives sharp. Sharper knives work as they are intended, dull ones will not.

If your dish tastes dull, add some salt (this includes baking!)

Adding some lemon juice will brighten a dish.

Sometimes, it is best to leave things alone. Steaks, burgers, other grill items will dry out when constantly flipped.

Do not put water on a grease fire. Cover with a pot lid or with baking soda.

Play with what you have in your spice cabinet.

A hot pan is identical to a cold one.

There are many great cooking shows to learn from! My favorite is Good Eats.

There are shows on cable, Netflix and other streaming services, but also on free sites like YouTube.

Start slowly with salt. You can always add more, but you cannot take it away.

Spice up frozen or packaged meals with fresh toppings. This works great with frozen pizza or ramen noodles.

Use high heat less than you think you should. Lower heat settings will cook the food without burning it.

Keep a scraps bowl on the countertop so you just throw all of them out at the end.

A meat thermometer is a great way to cook meat without drying or burning it.

A good-quality cooking item is worth the price.

  • You should invest in
  • a good quality knife
  • and a good quality pan.
  • NEVER use metal utensils on nonstick cookware.
